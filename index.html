Champana started as a question I couldn't stop asking myself: could I build a fashion brand entirely on my own — the concept, the fabric, the photography, the ads — with no funding and no team?

The founder
I designed every silhouette, sourced the linen, and briefed the atelier that cut and sewed each piece — outsourcing only the physical construction.
The marketer
I planned and ran every Meta campaign myself: targeting, creative testing, bidding, and budget, from launch through two and a half years of iteration.
The outcome
A registered, trademarked, self-funded brand that stayed profitable across its full run — and taught me the marketing funnel from the inside out.

Career

2016 – 2022Sales & E-Commerce Executive

From first hire to full-scale operations

Graduated in Finance & Banking (Hanoi Financial and Banking University) in 2016 and joined a household retail business as its very first employee, growing with it through formal incorporation in 2019 into a high-revenue distribution operation. The business ran a B2B2C model — sourcing from large-scale manufacturing brands and distributing to end consumers across multi-channel e-commerce. As the operation scaled into a multi-team structure, I coordinated its day-to-day: assigning and tracking 80+ tasks a day across the Seeding, Sales, and E-commerce teams on Base Wework, and helped carry its storefronts through peak-season pushes — including the 11.11.2022 mega sale, when the store passed one billion đồng in a single day, with the whole team packing on the warehouse floor into the night.

2022 – 2024Founder

Founding Champana — by the book

Registered the business in May 2022 and filed the Champana trademark within days of the licence — Certificate No. 517427, granted December 2024, protected in Class 35. Ran procurement formally too: VAT-invoiced fabric contracts with suppliers, including a ₫64.5M linen order for the 2023 collection.
